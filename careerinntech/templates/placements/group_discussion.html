Group Discussion Preparation

Lead, collaborate, and close discussions confidently.

A Group Discussion (GD) is a structured conversation where a panel evaluates how you communicate, reason, and collaborate in a team setting. You’re expected to contribute clear points, listen actively, and build on others’ ideas while maintaining respectful dialogue.

GD Formats

  • Case study discussions
  • Opinion-based debates
  • Abstract topics
  • Current affairs
  • Data interpretation rounds

Winning Moves

  • Strong opening statements
  • Support with facts
  • Bridge disagreements politely
  • Summarize key points
  • Invite quieter members

Evaluation Criteria

  • Clarity & confidence
  • Active listening
  • Team contribution
  • Logical flow
  • Respectful communication

Common Mistakes

  • Interrupting others
  • Repeating points
  • Speaking without structure

Preparation Plan

  • Read news daily
  • Practice 3–4 topics/week
  • Record & review sessions

Body Language

  • Open posture
  • Confident tone
  • Eye contact balance
